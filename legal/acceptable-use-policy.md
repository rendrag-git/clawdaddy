## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs your use of ClawDaddy's managed hosting services for OpenClaw AI assistants ("Service"). By using the Service, you agree to comply with this AUP.

## 2. Prohibited Activities

You may **not** use the Service for any of the following purposes:

### 2.1 Illegal Activities
- Any activity that violates local, state, federal, or international laws or regulations.
- Sharing, storing, or transmitting content that is illegal in your jurisdiction or ours (United States/Ohio).
- Violating intellectual property rights, including copyright, trademark, patent, or trade secret infringement.
- Harassment, stalking, threats, or any form of abusive behavior.

### 2.2 Harmful or Malicious Content
- Distributing malware, viruses, ransomware, or other harmful software.
- Phishing, spamming, or sending unsolicited bulk messages.
- Creating or distributing content that promotes violence, terrorism, or hate speech.
- Exploiting, endangering, or harming minors in any way.

### 2.3 Circumventing Service Limitations
- **Attempting to bypass API usage caps or rate limits.** This includes:
  - Manipulating usage reporting or monitoring systems
  - Using multiple accounts to avoid tier limitations
  - Deploying workarounds to exceed the $40/month API budget cap (Fully Managed tier)
- Reverse engineering, decompiling, or attempting to extract source code from the Service.
- Probing, scanning, or testing the vulnerability of our systems without prior written permission.

### 2.4 Reselling or Unauthorized Redistribution
- **Reselling, sublicensing, or redistributing access to the Service** without our prior written consent.
- Sharing your account credentials with others or allowing unauthorized users to access your account.
- Operating the Service in a "service bureau" or similar arrangement for the benefit of third parties without permission.

### 2.5 Infrastructure Abuse
- **Abusing or overloading our infrastructure**, including:
  - Excessive resource consumption that degrades service performance for other users
  - Running cryptocurrency mining operations
  - Launching denial-of-service attacks or similar disruptive activity
  - Automated scraping or data harvesting that places undue load on our systems
- Interfering with or disrupting the Service, our servers, or networks connected to the Service.

### 2.6 Fraudulent or Deceptive Practices
- Using stolen credit cards or payment methods.
- Creating accounts with false or misleading information.
- Impersonating any person or entity, or falsely claiming affiliation with any person or entity.

### 2.7 AI-Specific Prohibitions <!-- REVIEWER ADDITION: Without explicit AI use restrictions, customers could argue these uses weren't prohibited -->
- **Generating content that impersonates real individuals** without their consent, including deepfakes, synthetic voice cloning, or fabricated statements attributed to real people.
- **Using AI assistants to generate professional advice** (legal, medical, financial, therapeutic) and presenting it to third parties as professional advice without appropriate licensure and disclaimers.
- **Attempting to override, jailbreak, or circumvent AI model safety filters** or alignment mechanisms through prompt injection, prompt engineering attacks, or similar techniques.
- **Using the Service to develop or refine malware, exploits, or attack tools** through AI assistance.
- **Generating or attempting to generate CSAM** (child sexual abuse material) or any sexual content involving minors.
- **Using AI to conduct social engineering attacks**, create disinformation campaigns, or generate deceptive content intended to manipulate elections, financial markets, or public opinion.
- **Automated decision-making with real-world consequences** (e.g., hiring, lending, law enforcement) without human oversight and appropriate legal compliance.

### 2.8 Privacy Violations
- Collecting, storing, or sharing personal information of others without their consent.
- Violating the privacy rights of individuals or accessing data you are not authorized to access.
